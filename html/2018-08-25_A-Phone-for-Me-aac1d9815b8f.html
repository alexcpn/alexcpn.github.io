A Phone for Me

I am of Generation X. I learned this recently. Basically I am a person of the 70’s ;not so ancient, we are the ones who have been at the dawn of the digital world. We/Gen X have lived in a world without cell phones and internet and saw their wide popularity and growth when we were teens.

And yes it was the age where nothing was impossible, the spontaneous break up of the USSR, the TV shots of breaking the Berlin wall; and who can forget, watching real time the Iraqi night sky, lit up with anti-aircraft fire, the missile to missile fights, the Patriots against the ‘Scuds’…

The world was awakening to electronic music/synth-pop, the age of BoneyM and Abba,Modern Talking,Phil Colins, MJ, Stevie Wonder and closer to home A.R Rahman , Yanni and many more.. I spent my youngest years listening to my fathers tapes and then collection he bought, usually Grammy Award mixes, on my fathers trusty National Pansonic

It was the age of the, Walkman I too had one; I got it finally when I was in 8th, and I still remember,that feeling, the first song that I heard insulated from all, in a world of my own, in true stereo.

My first phone was a Nokia 3310. That I gifted my Mum on her birthday; it was after I got my first job. I used it myself for some time, though the number she still uses.

Was she surprised to hear the Ringtone and her birthday gift vibrating. It was heavy, but it lasted more years than I remember.You expected thing to last when you pay good money for it.

I moved out of my house and for myself I got another Nokia phone Nokia 3610. Nokia was so popular and had taken over Siemens phones; there was no confusion then what to buy.

I still have it; almost 15 years, or what remains of it. I guess it will still work with the old battery; if I could only find the charger. How thrilled was to get SMS at that time; and since SMS was charged, only meaningful messages were exchanged. I wish WhatsApp would start charging.

After this was camera phones, Nokia N series and the complexities and then the iPhone and lo the touch phone revolution along with Android phones, and Nokia phones disappeared. (I don’t dislike iPhone, but I hated the iTunes SW — yes I had the iPods, and did not want to do anything with the restrictive Apple OS)

Android was Open Source. I make a living writing and designing SW. Open Source is the ultimate virtual utopia, a land without borders, or currency, where everyone works for the others and everything is free and there is more and more of it.

Now I have this. Motorola Z Play.

This was after a lot of Motorola phones, starting with Moto Defy my first Android/ smart phone, Moto G (good phone), Moto M and then this. In this time I had Nokia 520 Windows phone and Xiomi Mi Note 4 for my wife.

I stayed away from bloat ware and Custom UI of Samsung and now the Xiomi phones;, pure Android was always what I liked and hence only Motorola for me.

Yes I wasted enormous time installing/upgrading/ trouble shooting Cynogenmod custom ROM on Moto Defy. I look back at all those wasted hours, and I remember this song..

“Time,I have been wasting time, 
Watching dreams go by …”

Maybe I am old; but I am hating all things now; I am feeling a love for my old Nokia Monochrome with the white LED.

It never let me down. I guess this ‘anti-fragility’ (thanks to Nassem Nicolas Taleb) and anti -complexity is what my heart desires now.

All these new phones die soon with SW upgrades; my one year relatively expensive Moto Z struggles to last till evening. I hate this feeling to be chained to chargers or battery packs. This lack of robustness, quality.

I hate the ever bigger screens that does not fit my pockets and occupies my one hand; the constant fear of misplacing or dropping it.

I am sick of this.

I used to have a small collection of cassette tapes and that was all the songs I loved and all the songs I enjoyed. Then came CD and then the MP3 and it was my dream come true; at least it seemed so. But the huge choices have made me eternally discontent. I long so much for that world where tapes were expensive and you learned to love what you have dreamed saved and bought

I am sick browsing and hearing all the hundreds of mostly junk and sometimes explicit songs to find that few great ones; most of the time I rarely enjoy; and most of the time I keep listening to the auto generated playlist of the different music apps , always in search for that golden tune.

Maybe this defines it most; my Moto Z Play is dying on me; I am sick and tired searching for a new phone every year; the whole purpose of a phone seems to be to search for new phones ; or spent hours addicted to browse useless reviews on YouTube.

Let me take it further. I have stayed away from Facebook and Orkut as I struggle just maintaining current social relationship, to keep tenons ghost of the past alive. But I have fallen for WhatsApp. It was almost inevitable. My carpenter started sending pictures in it. How could I not acknowledge such wide spread use. But that’s fine; till WhatsApp group came along and ruined everything.

I am sick of WhatsApp; and Twitter; and online comments, and the hate and stupidity and narrow mindedness of the online people that is thrown at your face; of a people who seem to deteriorate in rational thought and maybe intelligence, but more than ever in decency.

I am need of a new phone; I am in need always for something or the other.I understand the feeling of this digital addiction. I feel helpless sometimes. I have realized this; I am an addict to the digital economy of online shopping, sales and gadgets.It is a powerless feeling. I want to escape this.

I miss my old Nokia so much; or maybe that brief time where technology and Google was there; but limited.

I need a new phone; or maybe a new me, where I learn to be content with what I have, not to get distracted by choices.

I need to live in a new world where I learn to stay away , far away from the digital garbage and junk and walk on the pristine shores of an ideal,an ideal I yet don’t know what..

Maybe use my time and money for better use..
