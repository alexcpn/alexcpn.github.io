Risky vs Safe Coding: Roles of the Programmer, The Technical Leader and the Business

This article is about safe production quality programming practices, Feature Analysis and review, Design and Prototyping, Performance and Test Driven Development, Clean Code, Technical Debt, Pre-Commit Code Reviews, and how to manage this with the usual business requirements of fast features out.

The best programmers can do reckless programming, startups sometimes resort to this to implement their idea quickly.

In commercial SW development companies, this happens most of the time because nobody knows any better.

Poor design choices or technology choices can come back to haunt the SW product, gnawing away the profit and customer good will slowly till it is noticed. There are some metrics like Technical Debt that try to make this visible. In reality a lot of things in building a SW product cannot be easily measured.

Before we jump into the risk and safety practices, a little thought on why there is so less thought or attention given to this and other aspects of SW Development. Why are not the best educational institutions in the world not teaching about what to take care during commercial SW development than teaching Pascal or now Java programming or Object Oriented programming.

The Business Goal and SW Quality

The business goal is to get a product out fast with the minimum resources. The best written and the worst written product looks the same. Some amount of defects are acceptable, most business managers have very limited experience in SW development or releasing a successful product and maintaining it. By the time most of the defects of a released SW product is contained and customer goodwill that is lost is gained back, a lot of heads in the management side usually rolls. They are fired or sidelined because of the pain and cost that it has cost to the business.

A new and inexperienced set takes over usually for a new product.The cycle continues. This is not a funny thing. In a decade and a half as a programmer I have seen this happen so many times.This will happen again; someone has to take the blame eventually. Meanwhile business will clutch at any straw to save itself from drowning- Agile, Scrum , Lean, Incremental release , Continuous Release ... These can help, but the problem is always people first, what are the people motivated to do? What kind of people do you have?How are the goals and organisations structure set-up? Has the organisation changed to serve itself than the world ?

Personal Goals and SW Quality

Why do most people not think about things that are not immediate ? Human behaviour and thought is predominantly reactive ? There is very less retrospection other than that forced by pain (of failure or similar). When we choke with smog and our kids struggle to breathe,we bring in new laws. Unless the pain of not changing becomes intolerable, people rarely change. SW programmers , managers and all others are people. Nobody obsesses over something usually unless they have burned their fingers once. SW Quality is not pursued; just sufficient to scrape through is usually the unwritten target for everyone.

The cost of Poor Quality

I don’t think I need to write about the quality aspect of Toyota here. It has made the company the largest in the world. Almost all other car manufacture’s ape its practices (The Toyota Way) and have profited by it. Quality is profitable, in the long run. It is more than profit and loss. It adds a value to the brand that is priceless. Nokia phones anybody ? or Google Photos or Search or Linux.

Quality does not Come Free

Those who obsess about quality are almost always different from the rest. If there is one book that speaks more about it, it is the book Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance. One does not have to obsess over it,as to reach the zenith is almost impossible.

But there is still much in Quality that makes good long term business sense. Everyone knows this. Everyone pays lips service to this. But then there is a cost to this; and for a company under pressure, it takes a lot of leadership courage to hold on to this.

Product Delivery Process based on Quality First Principle

It is time to re imagine organisational structure that incentivize product quality over delivery quantity.

This may look counter intuitive. In the book that describes the Toyota Production System-The Toyota Way, they talk about how workers were incentivized to stop the production belt if they found some problem. Stopping a production chain in spite of targets is very counter intuitive. However till the root cause is analysed and either fixed if it was major or noted to be fixed later they did not move the belt. In essence they were forcing continuous improvement. For a car production company to slow down production means to take a near term hit in the number of cars produced and profitability.

You may need to go slow first to go fast with quality.

Business need to empower the SW developers and their leads and managers to bring out quality first and product second.

If the business is not aligned to this, and accepts this for the long term good,no other process will work.At the best there will be lot of faking and acting that goes along. For startups the long term aspect does no hold much value. But there is something endearing in a good SW product that wins customers. The secret sauce is quality and it holds a pretty good value even to those companies which think short term.

The role of the Technical Lead or Architect in SW Quality

There are lot of approaches to SW development and people fight over which is better; for example the type wars — Dynamic Typing versus Static Typing and so on. Usually the practices and technology depends on the context. The famous programmer and blogger Steve Yegge presents his thought that there is a political axis along which everyone is aligned one way or the other in SW development. Software conservatism and software liberalism, that is how he puts it in his article. Some parts are interesting

Just as in real-world politics, software conservatism and liberalism are radically different world views. Make no mistake: they are at odds. They have opposing value systems, priorities, core beliefs and motivations. These value systems clash at design time, at implementation time, at diagnostic time, at recovery time.
“We regard political conservatism as an ideological belief system that is significantly (but not completely) related to motivational concerns having to do with the psychological management of uncertainty and fear.”
Conservatism, at its heart, is really about risk management.

I hit upon Steve’s article while searching for this article.Most of what he writes about is based on this perceptive. However what I felt was missing in the article is, the context.

A SW Engineering architect has to play both the roles -conservative as well as liberal.

SW Practises and Quality First Thinking

It is extremely hard to change how the world thinks.The truth is that the world rarely thinks. The world just believes. And that world has the most educated to the least and everything in between. For anything that does not have strong scientific cause and effect known yet, the world will believe what the world believes.

The world may be right, it may be wrong, however that is not the point, the point is that most of the world never even considers it remotely possible that it can be wrong.

I have seen newly converted Agile believers fervently playing ‘planning poker’ with poker cards; companies spending millions on ‘Agile trainers’ to teach people how to play this game; the nonsense of putting stick-it notes on white boards and running after those post-its when it flies off or some other practise. I may have angered someone here; hold on before you comment,I could give much better examples; but I believe in writing non dangerously for myself; so I am sticking with hurting a few agile believers ( I am also one, it is sometimes good to think if all the rituals we do in the pursuit of an idea is correct); I will leave it for the world to ponder. I am a conservative in writing though a liberal at heart.

In his article Steve paints people into mostly two colours, liberals who embrace dynamic typing and not being afraid of bugs and Conservative who shudder at the thought of bugs and will embrace static typed languages. What he misses is the context; why you need to be Liberal sometimes and Conservative at other times.

Brave like a lion, gentle like a lamb..

SW Quality and the Passionate Programmer

For some, a job is a fulfilment of a part of life, for many others it is a business relation. Both are fine; sometimes you find same people morphing into one or the other based on external factors like time, age, project or a bad day at home or a bad patch.

Like every field or organisation on earth,SW development needs both these people.

For inspired SW developers every language is beautiful in a way. They can write fast and fix fast.

They usually follow their own safety nets;being a SW Conservative Lead or Architect,and telling this inspired person that their unit test coverage is not as per the company stipulated rule is asking for murder.

They will usually tell you why it is a waste to write those test in that context. This is why, in this business, we need Leads who can distinguish those inspired from the commercial, and manage one without driving the other out.

There are certain things and rules we can bludgeon in with less resistance to all; try doing this for everything ( coverage threshold, test automation..) and you will soon be left with those who do not care, and say Yes to everything.

It is the inspired few that always inspires the rest and drives things. This is why most business want to be in the start-up sweet spot.

For a least inspired SW developer, who has no passion towards work, but one among the millions,being a SW liberal Tech Lead and not checking or enforcing unit tests and other code safety rules is being insanely stupid.You will get the worst quality and technical debt in no time.

Grooming better Leaders in SW industry

The SW business is currently structured that there is very less emphasis on technical excellence. Business does not know the value of code structuring. The emphasis is on producing things fast as they are not able to see the presence or absence of Quality till pretty late.

People align very fast to what is expected out of them.

A big company can get infected pretty fast with the wrong expectation propagated everywhere.

The few who see beyond are thought to be either odd or rebellious or stupid or conservative or some other label. The best of them leave, the others stop caring after sometime.

Leads and leaders tend to align with business expectation thinking that those higher up will surely know better. There are very few leaders ready to walk and talk with the crowds or put their ear to the ground. This has to change. We need leaders who understand the work and workers deeply; who are aligned with SW quality.

Summary

It is best to summarise with a exaggerated plot.

The plot begins with a dilemma. The business needs the product as fast a possible. The inspired few has to direct the work of a lot of commercial programmers. The business tells him product quality matters a lot. He tells that product quality is related very much to code quality and related like design decision and external library decisions. However all the business hears is blah-blah-blah. However they trust the person and ask him/her to do what he/she has to do. This seems like a happy scenario, however the truth is that this happiness is short lived.

Days pass to months; the business get’s no features, no product yet. The question is posed to the inspired. The inspired tells the code is dangerous. I am managing the risk of the reckless programmer who are not aware of their recklessness yet. And the words came tumbling out, analysis and design prototype, analysis review, code quality, test driven development, pre-commit code review and the works.

The business is confused, it goes and searches the net and finds articles upon articles.

Analysis and review are bureaucratic and slow things down.

Unit tests are overrated,Code quality is overrated.

Software conservatism is the doom of the industry.

The informed business comes again to the inspired. We really trust you, but please don’t obsess over quality. We will let you, when we have time, but as you know we are on a burning platform, if we don’t ship we sink.

The inspired does not have the nerve to speak back to business, that to be a software liberal, in a commercial software house is almost suicidal; that he is not obsessing over quality, but just the bare necessity.

He looses sleep as he loves the business and knows that building a product with infinite bug way or bugs as a feature way will sap all profit out of the product, that armies of more commercial programmers have to be recruited and maintained to keep the bug count without incurring penalty.

Is there any other way out ? Is enforcing code quality the real problem. No, enforcing this on the uninspired unskilled inexperienced programmers that is the majority is. Can he use the Scrum Master role to whip the Scrum Team to build faster? Maybe, but all creativity will be destroyed, all responsibility gets transferred to the master and it becomes the full time job of the master. Can we deliver a beta quality software faster, so that expectations are met but not very high ? He tosses around and finally sleeps.

In the morning he drives to The Office and agrees to all what the business ask him. He agrees to let the code flow out faster. Everyone is happy now. The happiness lasts a lot longer now. Everyone forgets about him soon, there is celebration that the product is out and it is showing itself to be a success.He knows that surely he will be in limelight again, as the bug slayer hero, the fire fighting crusader, the hero of the game.

And then it happens; one incident after the other. He leads the fire fighting, makes everyone happy. Business loves him, have always loved him, they shower praises on him.

He sleeps a bit troubled now; but he has tried his best, there was no other way out. There is too much ignorance around. Years hence, he knows, everyone will look back to this primitive dinosaur era and shudder. Things will be better then.With that hope, he shuts his weary eyes and sleeps.
